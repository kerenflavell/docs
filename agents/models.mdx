### Selection guidelines by use case

- **Development & terminal work** → **Claude 3.5 Sonnet**\
  Great at code, debugging, large codebases, and terminal integration.
- **Research & deep analysis** → **Claude 3 Opus** or **GPT-4 Turbo**\
  Premium reasoning and large context for extensive literature or data review.
- **High-volume workflows** → **Claude 3 Haiku** or **GPT-4o Mini**\
  Rapid responses at penny-level prices; perfect for bulk Executor runs.
- **Budget-conscious deployments** → **GPT-3.5 Turbo** or **Claude 3 Haiku**\
  Cheapest token rates for straightforward Q&A or routine assistance.
- **Multimodal (images \+ text)** → **GPT-4o** (or Turbo)\
  Native vision support with solid reasoning.

### Cost-optimization playbook

1. **Hybrid tiering**
   - _Premium_ (Seer / Director): Claude Opus, GPT-4
   - _Balanced_ (Supervisor / Assistant): Claude 3.5 Sonnet, GPT-4o
   - _Economy_ (Executor): Claude Haiku, GPT-4o Mini
2. **Context sizing**
   - Choose **Claude (200 k)** for memory-heavy roles.
   - Use **GPT (128 k)** where smaller context suffices.

**Tip:** Qui Studio auto-discovers new provider models as they launch, so you can upgrade characters later without touching your existing role configs or hierarchies.
